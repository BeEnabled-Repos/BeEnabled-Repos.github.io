Meet Jen

It wasn't her ability to solve the problem at hand, it was the spaces and tools around her.

Our founder, Jen, has struggled with the use of her hands – limited dexterity – for her entire life. In college, she had aspirations to become a software developer. Typing and writing were exhausting, but Jen made it work, until she couldn't.

During her second year of college, Jen acquired an illness that left her with good use of four of her fingers. Like 11.7 million Americans with limited dexterity, Jen's body could no longer keep up with her brain.

A recent Camelback Ventures Fellow, Ohio State University alumna and President's Prize winner, Jen is a serial innovator working to design a more accessible world.
